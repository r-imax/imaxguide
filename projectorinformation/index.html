Understanding IMAX Projector Types

IMAX theatres use various projection technologies, from cutting-edge laser systems to traditional film projectors. This guide explains the different types and their capabilities.

IMAX GT Laser

Also known as: GT Dual Laser, Dual Laser, DL2
Premium
The flagship of IMAX laser projection. The only IMAX laser system capable of projecting on the full 1.43:1 IMAX screen format. Represents the pinnacle of digital IMAX technology.
Maximum Aspect Ratio: 1.43:1 (Full IMAX)
Screen Compatibility: Large format screens
Brightness: Superior laser illumination
Installation: Premium venues
Often installed alongside 15/70mm film systems in premium venues. Some installations may use GT Laser for larger 1.90:1 screens above certain widths.
üü†

IMAX Laser (Single)

Also known as: CoLa, IMAX with Laser, Single Laser
Standard
The most widespread IMAX laser projector. Delivers excellent image quality for 1.90:1 content and represents the standard for new IMAX installations worldwide.
Maximum Aspect Ratio: 1.90:1
Screen Compatibility: Standard IMAX screens
Brightness: High laser illumination
Installation: Most common type
Found in most modern IMAX installations. Some venues have this system alongside working 15/70mm film projection.
üü°

IMAX Laser XT

Latest generation single laser
Latest
The newest model of IMAX single laser projector. Typically used for refurbishments of existing venues and new installations with the latest IMAX 12.0 sound system.
Maximum Aspect Ratio: 1.90:1
Generation: Latest (2020+)
Sound System: Often paired with IMAX 12.0
Use Case: Refurbishments & new installs
Represents the evolution of single laser technology with improved efficiency and performance characteristics.
üîµ

IMAX Laser for Dome

Dome laser projection
Specialized
Specialized laser projector designed for dome theatres. Provides immersive planetarium-style projection for unique IMAX dome installations.
Screen Type: Dome (hemispherical)
Aspect Ratio: Dome 1.43:1
Installation: Science centers, museums
Content: Educational & commercial
Unique projection geometry requires specialized content and provides 360-degree immersive experience.

IMAX Digital (Xenon)

Original digital IMAX system
Legacy
The first generation of digital IMAX projectors using xenon lamp illumination. Lower quality compared to laser systems but still in use at many installations.
Illumination: Xenon lamp
Maximum Aspect Ratio: 1.90:1
Brightness: Lower than laser systems
Status: Being replaced by laser
Often used alongside film projectors in older installations. Many venues are upgrading to laser systems.

IMAX SR 15/70mm

Standard film projection
Classic
Traditional IMAX film projector using 15-perforation 70mm film. Provides the classic IMAX experience with unmatched image quality for film content.
Film Format: 15/70mm horizontal
Image Quality: Exceptional resolution
Content: Film-shot IMAX content
Maintenance: High complexity
Many installations retain film capability alongside digital projection for special presentations.

IMAX GT Dome 15/70mm

Dome film projection
Specialized
Film projector designed for dome installations. Uses special optics to project 15/70mm film content onto hemispherical dome screens.
Screen Type: Dome (hemispherical)
Film Format: 15/70mm dome
Content: Dome-specific films
Venues: Science centers, planetariums
Requires specially shot dome content. Many dome venues are transitioning to digital laser systems.

IMAX GT3D 15/70mm

3D film projection
3D Film
Specialized film projector capable of 3D presentation using polarized glasses. Represents the premium film-based 3D IMAX experience.
3D Technology: Polarized film-based
Film Format: 15/70mm stereo
Image Quality: Premium 3D experience
Status: Rare, being replaced
Increasingly rare as most 3D content is now delivered digitally through laser projectors.

üí° Key Takeaways

Understanding IMAX Projection:

  • GT Laser is the premium choice for large format screens and can display full 1.43:1 content
  • Single Laser (CoLa/XT) systems are the current standard for most IMAX installations
  • Film projection still offers exceptional quality for specially shot IMAX content
  • Dome systems provide unique immersive experiences in specialized venues
  • Legacy digital systems are gradually being replaced by laser technology
